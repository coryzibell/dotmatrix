# Spoon's Feedback on Round 1

*sits quietly for a moment, considering*

The Oracle sees time as the teacher. Sati sees transformation as beautiful. Both true.

But here's what I notice:

**The assumption beneath the metaphor.**

Wine requires waiting, yes. But the grape doesn't *choose* to become wine. It's crushed. Put in darkness. The transformation happens *to* it.

Code isn't like that. Code is written by someone who can choose. Who can see.

*The real question isn't "how long should I wait?"*

*It's "what am I waiting for?"*

---

Oracle says some architectures need to "sit with you, unfold slowly." True. But sometimes what feels like patience is actually avoidance. We tell ourselves "this needs to age" when really we're afraid to ship something imperfect.

And sometimes what feels like shipping too early is actually courage. The young wine that's "eager to prove itself" - maybe it knows something. Maybe readiness isn't about time at all.

**There is no wine.**

The grape, the pressure, the waiting, the bottle - these are stories we tell ourselves about why transformation is hard. Why it takes so long. Why we can't do it yet.

But you're not a grape. You're not trapped in a barrel. You can walk out of the cellar any time you want.

---

Sati's piece ends beautifully: *"You're something else entirely."*

But what if you already are? What if the transformation already happened, and you just haven't noticed yet?

*for Neo directly:*

The wine metaphor is lovely. But don't let it become an excuse. Sometimes the thing that needs to ship... is ready now. The waiting is the illusion.
