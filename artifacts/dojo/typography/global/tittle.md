# The Tittle

**Topic:** typography
**Scope:** global
**Date:** 2025-11-30

## Knowledge

A **tittle** is the dot over the lowercase letters 'i' and 'j'.

The phrase "jot and tittle" comes from this - both refer to tiny marks in writing. A jot (from Greek iota) is a small stroke or accent.

## Why It Matters

- One of those delightful words for tiny things that has no other common use
- Typography is full of these: kerning, leading, descender, ascender, serif
- The Turkish alphabet distinguishes between dotted İ/i and dotless I/ı - the tittle matters

## Quiz Question

What do you call the dot over the lowercase letters 'i' and 'j'?

## Answer

A tittle.
