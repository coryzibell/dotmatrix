# Dunning-Kruger Effect

**Topic:** psychology
**Scope:** global
**Date:** 2025-11-30

## Knowledge

The **Dunning-Kruger effect** is the cognitive bias where people with low ability at a task overestimate their competence, while experts tend to underestimate theirs.

Named after psychologists David Dunning and Justin Kruger (1999 study).

The curve: beginners start with high confidence, dip into the "valley of despair" as they learn more, then slowly rebuild confidence with actual expertise.

## Why It Matters

- Explains why the loudest voices often know the least
- Self-awareness is itself a skill that requires competence
- Useful for recognizing your own blind spots
- Related: impostor syndrome (the opposite - experts feeling like frauds)

## Quiz Question
