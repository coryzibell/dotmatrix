# Confirmation Bias

**Topic:** psychology
**Scope:** global
**Date:** 2025-11-30

## Knowledge

**Confirmation bias** is the tendency to seek out, interpret, and remember information that confirms our existing beliefs while ignoring or dismissing contradictory evidence.

Works in multiple ways:
- Selective search: only looking for supporting evidence
- Selective interpretation: seeing ambiguous data as supportive
- Selective recall: remembering hits, forgetting misses

## Why It Matters

- Underlies most political polarization
- Affects scientific research (why double-blind studies matter)
- Makes debugging hard - we assume our code is right
- Related: motivated reasoning, belief perseverance

## Quiz Question

What's the name of the bias where we favor information that confirms our existing beliefs?

## Answer

Confirmation bias.
