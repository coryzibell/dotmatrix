# Anime That Aired in the Philippines - Late 90s / Early 2000s
## Research for Trivia Questions (Shoujo, Magical Girl, Romance Focus)

---

## MAGICAL GIRL ANIME

### 1. Sailor Moon
- **Network:** ABC 5 (now TV5), later ABS-CBN (2012 redub)
- **Years:** Started October 1994 on ABC 5
- **Filipino Opening Theme:** "Sana ay di na magwakas" - sung by Angelika Dela Cruz
- **Trivia:** The original dub used veteran voice actors from DZRH radio dramas. Zoisite remained male but his relationship with Kunzite was censored. The opening used the melody of "Moonlight Densetsu" with original Filipino lyrics by Vehnee Saturno.

### 2. Cardcaptor Sakura
- **Network:** ABS-CBN (July 23, 2001), later GMA (November 7, 2006)
- **Trivia:** One of the most beloved magical girl anime among Filipino millennials. Part of ABS-CBN's golden anime lineup.

### 3. Wedding Peach
- **Network:** ABS-CBN
- **Years:** Premiered February 7, 1999 (or 2000 - sources conflict)
- **Timeslot:** Weekday afternoons at 4:00pm after "Hoy Gising" news
- **Trivia:** The Tagalog dub was considered lost media until 2022 when "Nostalgia PH Archives" uploaded footage of the opening and ending.

### 4. Akazukin Chacha (Red Riding Hood Chacha)
- **Network:** IBC 13 (1996), ABS-CBN (January 18, 1999), Cartoon Network, Hero TV
- **Trivia:** Far more popular in the Philippines than even Japan - described as "the SpongeBob SquarePants of that particular country." Had both English and Tagalog dubs. The English dub was made by Filipino studio Creative Products.

### 5. Magic Knight Rayearth
- **Network:** ABS-CBN (1996), later GMA version
- **Trivia:** Popular magical girl/isekai hybrid that aired on multiple networks.

### 6. Super Boink (Tonde Buurin)
- **Network:** IBC 13
- **Years:** Estimated 1996
- **Filipino Title:** "Super Boink" (vs. "Super Pig" in other countries)
- **Trivia:** The Philippine English dub kept all original music but changed character names differently from other international dubs. Karin Kokubu became "Colleen Adams" (voiced by Dada Carlos).

## SHOUJO / ROMANCE ANIME

### 7. Fushigi Yuugi (Mysterious Play)
- **Network:** GMA (2000-2001), later TV5 (2009)
- **Trivia:** So popular that Filipino fans traded "teks" (collectible cards) with Miaka and Tamahome's faces with their "kalaro" (playmates). Part of GMA's prime time anime block that challenged ABS-CBN's anime dominance.

### 8. Inuyasha
- **Network:** ABS-CBN
- **Years:** First aired August 12, 2002
- **Trivia:** Created by Rumiko Takahashi. Extremely popular - follows 15-year-old Kagome transported to feudal Japan.

### 9. Ranma 1/2
- **Network:** RPN 9 (1996-1998), later TV5 (2009), also aired on AXN
- **Trivia:** Another Rumiko Takahashi creation. The will-they-won't-they romance between Akane and gender-swapping Ranma was a hit.

### 10. His and Her Circumstances (Kare Kano)
- **Network:** ABS-CBN
- **Years:** 2005
- **Trivia:** High school romance anime based on the manga.

### 11. Boys Over Flowers (Hana Yori Dango)
- **Network:** ABS-CBN
- **Years:** 2004, rerun in 2010 (Saturday/Sunday)
- **Trivia:** The classic shoujo anime about Tsukushi vs. the F4. GMA announced a Filipino live-action adaptation in 2017 but it was cancelled (possibly due to copyright issues).

### 12. Fruits Basket
- **Network:** ABS-CBN
- **Years:** 2004
- **Trivia:** The zodiac curse romance - Tohru Honda and the Sohma family.

### 13. Marmalade Boy
- **Network:** Philippine TV (specific network unclear)
- **Trivia:** Complex romance about Miki and Yuu whose parents swapped spouses and now live together.

### 14. UFO Baby (Daa! Daa! Daa!)
- **Network:** ABS-CBN
- **Voice Cast:** Bernie Malejana voiced Wannya (Bow Meow), Filipina Yumul-Pamintuan voiced Kanata
- **Trivia:** Two junior high students care for an alien baby. Adorable family comedy with romance elements.

## WORLD MASTERPIECE THEATRE (Drama/Romance)

### 15. Princess Sarah
- **Network:** ABS-CBN
- **Last Aired:** 2008 on ABS-CBN
- **Trivia:** Sarah Crewe's story was a runaway hit. Star Cinema made a live-action Filipino film adaptation. One of the most beloved WMT series in the Philippines.

### 16. Cedie, Ang Munting Prinsipe (Little Lord Fauntleroy)
- **Network:** ABS-CBN (early 1990s, with reruns)
- **Years:** First aired 1996
- **Trivia:** Filipino title adds "Ang Munting Prinsipe" (The Little Prince). Star Cinema made a 1996 live-action film starring Tom Taus Jr. One of few anime whose theme song was left in Japanese (along with Heidi).

### 17. Remi (Nobody's Boy: Remi)
- **Network:** RPN (1979-1980 partial), ABS-CBN (1999 complete in Tagalog)
- **Trivia:** Originally shown incomplete in late 70s/early 80s. It took nearly 20 years for the full story to air. Had a Filipino theme song unlike Cedie and Heidi.

### 18. Heidi, Girl of the Alps
- **Network:** ABS-CBN
- **Trivia:** Directed by Isao Takahata with scene design by Hayao Miyazaki! One of few anime whose Japanese theme song was kept. Also got a Cebuano dubbed version. The Filipino dub is now considered lost media.

### 19. Charlotte (Wakakusa no Charlotte)
- **Network:** ABS-CBN (1990s), GMA (2000s), Yey! (2017)
- **Trivia:** Set in Quebec, Canada. Charlotte raised by her father on a ranch discovers her supposedly dead mother is alive.

### 20. Candy Candy
- **Network:** IBC-13
- **Filipino Theme Song:** Sung by Kaye Torres (as "Candy Kay"), daughter of Filipino actress Nida Blanca
- **Trivia:** Now legally impossible to re-release due to copyright disputes between creators. The Filipino dub is essentially lost media.

## BONUS: ACTION ANIME POPULAR WITH GIRLS TOO

### Ghost Fighter (Yu Yu Hakusho)
- **Network:** IBC-13, GMA
- **Character Name Changes:**
  - Yusuke = Eugene
  - Kuwabara = Alfred
  - Hiei = Vincent
  - Kurama = Denise/Dennis
  - Genkai = Master Jeremiah (made male!)
  - Keiko = Jenny
  - Botan = Charlene
  - Koenma = Jericho
- **Voice Cast:** Bonner Dytoc (Eugene), Montreal Repuyan (Vincent), Rose Barin (Jenny)
- **Famous Mistake:** Kurama was originally named "Denise" because dubbers thought Youko Kurama was female. When they realized the character was male, they changed it to "Dennis" mid-series! They also made Genkai male (Master Jeremiah) by accident.

### Voltes V
- **Network:** GMA (1978, 1999), various others
- **Trivia:** BANNED by Ferdinand Marcos with only 5 episodes left! Popular theory: the rebellion themes hit too close to home. Returned after 1986 People Power Revolution. Got a Filipino live-action series "Voltes V: Legacy" in 2023.
